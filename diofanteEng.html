Context of the problem

God granted Diophantus to spend the sixth part of his life in youth; a twelfth, in adolescence; a seventh then was drained into a sterile marriage. Five more years passed, after which a son was born. But this son—wretched and yet beloved! — he had barely reached half his father's age, he died. Diophantus spent another four years, mitigating his own pain with the study of the science of numbers, before reaching the end of his existence. It is possible that Diophantus, concerned with solving the indeterminate problems of Arithmetic, had not considered obtaining the perfect solution to King Hieron's problem, which is not indicated in his work.

Resolution

The Diophantus Problem or Epitaph of Diophantus can be solved with the help of a 1st degree equation with one unknown.

x = Diophantus' age at death
x/6 = Childhood
x/12 = Adolescence
x/7 = Sterile marriage
5 years later his son was born
x/2 = Lifespan of your child
4 years Diophantus suffered before his death
  x = x/6 + x/12 + x/7 + 5 + x/2 + 4 (first degree equation)
x - x/6 - x/12 - x/7 - x/2 = 9
9x = 756
x = 756/9 = 84

Riddle answer: 84 years old
