Context of the problem

In the book “The Man Who Calculated”, the main character, Beremiz Samir, is challenged to solve a mathematical problem involving a chessboard.
The story told begins with the legend of the creation of the game of chess, where the game's inventor, Lahur Sessa, presented the game he invented to the king. Where the sovereign saw, played and loved.

He was so pleased that he offered Sessa whatever he wanted as a reward: gold, land or even a palace. But Sessa asked the king for a reward that appeared to be simple: a grain of wheat for the first square on the board, two grains for the second, four for the third, and so on, doubling the amount of grain for each square until the 64th. home.

Resolution

To solve this problem, we will need to calculate the total amount of wheat grains that the king would have to give to the inventor. The grain sequence is a geometric progression where each term is twice the previous one. The formula for the sum of the terms of a geometric progression is:

Sn = a ⋅ r −1(rn−1)

Being:
Sn - is the sum of the n terms.
a - is the first term of the progression (in this case, 1 grain).
r - is the ratio of the progression (in this case, 2).
n - is the number of terms (in this case, 64).

Substituting the values ​​into the formula, we will have:

S64 = 1 ⋅ 2 −1(264−1)
Since (2 - 1 = 1), the formula simplifies to:
S64 = 264 −1

Calculating (2^{64}):
264 = 18,446,744,073,709,551,616

Therefore:
S64 = 18,446,744,073,709,551,615

Therefore, the king would have to give the inventor 18,446,744,073,709,551,615 grains of wheat, an extremely high amount.
