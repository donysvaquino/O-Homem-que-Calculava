Context of the problem

While undergoing his trial, Beremiz was interrogated by some wise men. The third wise man who should interrogate Beremiz was the famous astronomer Abul-Hassã Ali de Alcalá, coming from Baghdad at the invitation of Caliph Al-Motacém. The wise man asks Beremiz: "Is it possible, in Mathematics, to remove a false rule from a true property?" Beremiz then responds to the wise man with the following thought:

"Let us admit that a curious algebraist wanted to determine the square root of a four-digit number. We know that the square root of a number is another number that, multiplied by itself even, gives a product equal to the given number. Let's also suppose that the algebraist, freely taking three numbers to his liking, highlighted the following numbers: 2,025, 3,025 and 9,801".

Resolution

We know that the square root of a number is another number multiplied by itself. Calculating the square root of 2025, we would have 45, that is, 45x45 is equal to 2025. It is possible to observe that 45 is the sum of 20+25, which are the parts of the number 2025 when a point is placed between 20 and 25= 20.25 .

Thus, we can conclude that to know the square root of a 4-digit number, it is necessary to divide the number into two classes using a point, and then add them together, obtaining the square root of the number.
